## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Cold-start latency and token efficiency are the unglamorous bottlenecks everyone is quietly attacking — the gap between "model works in a notebook" and "model is cheap in production" is still enormous, and today's posts show teams reaching for exotic tooling (FUSE, CUDA checkpointing, LP solvers) to close it.
- [Cutting inference cold starts by 40x with LP, FUSE, C/R, and CUDA-checkpoint](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48183038) — 65pts — the 40x headline is audacious; worth reading to see which part of that stack is doing the real work

**agent-systems**: The agent layer is fragmenting into specialization — search, deployment, and identity are all getting purpose-built primitives rather than being bolted onto existing tools, suggesting the "LLM + bash" era is giving way to proper agent infrastructure.
- [Agora-1: The Multi-Agent World Model](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48183748) — 71pts — multi-agent world models are the step between "agents that use tools" and "agents that simulate consequences"; worth watching the architecture claims carefully

**Deep dives**:
- [The Fil-C Optimized Calling Convention](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48162876) — memory-safe C with a custom calling convention is a rare systems deep-dive; relevant to anyone thinking about safe inference runtimes
- [Voice AI Systems Are Vulnerable to Hidden Audio Attacks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48178378) — with voice AI proliferating rapidly, adversarial audio attacks are the new prompt injection and this is an early look at the threat model
- [Linux security mailing list 'almost unmanageable'](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48178692) — the foundation that everything including ML infra runs on is showing governance cracks; the "who maintains the maintainers" question has no good answer yet
