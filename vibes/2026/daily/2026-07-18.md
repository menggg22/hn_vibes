## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The Kimi K3 release is the gravitational center, but the real infrastructure story today is evaluation methodology — HN is interrogating whether benchmarks mean anything anymore, with the pelican benchmark thread and the evaluation inconsistency post both asking the same underlying question: how do we actually know which model is better?
- [Kimi K3, and what we can still learn from the pelican benchmark](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48947717) — 250pts — Worth reading for the honest reckoning with whether any benchmark survives contact with frontier models; the "what we can still learn" framing is doing real work.

**agent-systems**: Multiple builders shipped agent memory and coordination tools today (Wolbarg, Crux, Hearth), but the most revealing signal is the on-chain AI bond market — when agents start issuing financial instruments, the question of agent identity and accountability becomes urgent rather than theoretical.
- [Show HN: On-chain bond market where the issuers are AI agents](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48947754) — 14pts — The interesting question isn't the crypto angle; it's what legal and financial infrastructure assumes about the humans behind instruments, and what happens when there aren't any.

**Deep dives**:
- [Evidence of inconsistencies in evaluation process and selection of winners](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48946010) — 434pts 268c — Read this alongside the Kimi K3 release; if evaluation processes are compromised, every frontier leaderboard position is suspect and the implications for AI procurement are large.
