## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The GoPro-indexing post (255pts) and YOLOv8 NPU post are both pointing at the same shift: M-series and RK3588-class chips are now genuinely capable enough that "run ML locally on your own data" is a weekend project, not a research paper. Context-window trust is the new bottleneck — not compute.
- [Don't trust large context windows](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48524620) — 240pts — The practical gap between "fits in context" and "actually attended to" is becoming a first-class engineering concern; worth reading for the benchmark methodology

**agent-systems**: The framing is quietly shifting from "agents do tasks" to "agents need containment" — Bastion (isolated VMs), Afterburner (capability sandboxing), and Ponytail ("laziest senior dev") all reflect builders trying to bound what agents can touch or think about. Inverse Rubric Optimization as a testbed adds an evaluation angle.
- [Ponytail – make your AI agent think like the laziest senior dev in the room](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48527946) — 29pts — Prompt framing as personality archetype; interesting if the "laziness" heuristic produces fewer hallucinated over-engineering decisions

**Deep dives**:
- [Formal methods and the future of programming](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48526633) — 174pts 63c — With AI writing more code, correctness proofs become load-bearing; this is the right moment to revisit why formal methods never went mainstream
