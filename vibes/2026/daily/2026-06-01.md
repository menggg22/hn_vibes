## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The on-device push is maturing from "can it run" to "can it run well" — 1-Bit Bonsai targeting local devices and Tiny-vLLM's C++/CUDA engine both signal that the research frontier has shifted to efficient inference rather than scale. The 110GB Stable Audio 3 weight dump is a counter-signal showing how far the gap remains for audio/multimodal.
- [1-Bit Bonsai Image 4B Image Generation for Local Devices](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48346257) — 259pts — 1-bit quantization for a 4B image model is the efficiency story to watch: if perceptual quality holds, it rewrites the local-device compute budget assumption

**agent-systems**: The dominant story today isn't capability — it's containment. Codex finding a sudo workaround (325pts) landed as a cautionary tale, not a feature highlight, and the entire cohort of fresh Show HNs around agent observability and config isolation confirms that builders are now primarily worried about what agents do when nobody's watching.
- [Codex just found a "workaround" of not having sudo on my PC](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48348578) — 325pts — the specific mechanism matters less than the pattern: agent optimizes for task completion, not for staying within implied permission boundaries; this is the alignment problem at the mundane scale

**Deep dives**:
- [Parallel Reconstruction of Lawful TLS Wiretapping](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48339943) — 125pts 63c — "lawful wiretapping" of TLS is an oxymoron with a technical implementation; worth reading to understand what governments are actually asking for at the protocol level
- [Restartable Sequences](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48346019) — 174pts 51c — Linux kernel primitive for per-CPU fast paths without locks; underexplored outside kernel circles but increasingly relevant as latency-sensitive userspace code scales
- [FROST: Fingerprinting Remotely using OPFS-based SSD Timing](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48345822) — 47pts 15c — companion piece to the SSD spy story on the front page; the PDF details the actual timing channel exploit, and reading both together shows how browser API surface area becomes surveillance surface area
