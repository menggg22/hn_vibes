## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Memory cost dominance is the infrastructure story of the moment — two-thirds of AI chip BOM being memory means every efficiency bet is now really a memory architecture bet. Meanwhile DeepSeek's aggressive caching strategy (highlighted in the Reasonix post) suggests the near-term optimization frontier is cache hit rate, not raw compute.
- [Memory has grown to nearly two-thirds of AI chip component costs](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48258684) — 266pts — worth reading to understand why HBM scarcity is now the binding constraint on every AI hardware roadmap, not just FLOPS

**agent-systems**: Skepticism is sharpening from vague unease into specific, technical critiques. Two high-scoring posts today attack different failure modes: backend code generation reliability and over-reliance on LLMs for architectural decisions. The community is moving from "agents are flaky" to "here is precisely how and why they fail."
- [Constraint Decay: The Fragility of LLM Agents in Back End Code Generation](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48256912) — 156pts — names a specific, reproducible failure mode (constraints degrading over multi-step generation) that every backend agent builder should stress-test against
- [Claude is not your architect. Stop letting it pretend](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48259784) — 223pts — the title alone is doing work; read for the specific examples of where LLM architectural advice diverges from production reality

**Deep dives**:
- [Why is Vivado 2026.1 dropping Linux support for free tier?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48254309) — 294pts, 174c — Xilinx/AMD quietly closing off the free FPGA development path on Linux; the implications for open-source hardware development and hobbyist FPGA work are significant and underreported
- [I spent 50 hours drawing a line graph](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48223997) — 404pts — a masterclass in the obsessive craft of data visualization; worth reading as a counterargument to AI-generated charts becoming the default
- [Defeating Git Rigour Fatigue with Jujutsu](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48259861) — 74pts — practical walkthrough of using jj to escape the cognitive overhead of Git's staging model; timely as jj adoption accelerates in serious engineering orgs
