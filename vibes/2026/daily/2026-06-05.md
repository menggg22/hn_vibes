## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The theme today is efficiency at multiple levels — KV-cache quantization, encoder-free multimodal models, and QKV projection research all point toward the same pressure: serving capable models cheaply at scale. Gemma 4 going encoder-free is the most architecturally significant signal.
- [Gemma 4 12B: A unified, encoder-free multimodal model](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48385906) — 1011pts — dropping the vision encoder entirely in a competitive 12B model is worth understanding; what does that trade off and where does it win?
- [KVarN: Native vLLM backend for KV-cache quantization by Huawei](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48399974) — 111pts — Huawei shipping production-grade vLLM infrastructure is itself a geopolitical signal wrapped in an infra post
- [Do transformers need three projections? Systematic study of QKV variants](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48405931) — 41pts — low score but high signal; ablation studies on attention projections are rare and could influence architecture choices for anyone training from scratch

**agent-systems**: The field is bifurcating — hype-heavy "recursive self-improvement" narratives on one side, pragmatic cost/tooling infrastructure on the other. The NSA-Anthropic story introduces a new player: nation-state operators as agent users.
- [When AI Builds Itself: Our progress toward recursive self-improvement](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48400842) — 284pts — the comment-to-score ratio tells you the claims are being contested hard; worth reading the skeptical comments as much as the post
- [NSA using Anthropic's Mythos for cyber attacks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48404233) — 68pts — if accurate, this is the first named government offensive use of a frontier model's named internal system; the implications for dual-use AI policy are significant
- [Anthropic's open-source framework for AI-powered vulnerability discovery](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48403980) — 220pts — open-sourcing offensive security tooling while also (per the NSA story) having that tooling used offensively creates an interesting same-day tension

**Deep dives**:
- [Learn SQL Once, Use It for 30 Years](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48347483) — counterweight to the AI tooling churn; worth reading today specifically because the Berkeley grades story raises the question of what foundational skills are actually worth protecting
- [UK media fails to disclose defence sector links in nearly 60% of cases](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48395938) — directly relevant to evaluating any AI/security coverage, including today's NSA-Anthropic story; epistemics post disguised as media criticism
