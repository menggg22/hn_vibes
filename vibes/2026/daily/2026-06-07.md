## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The big infrastructure story today isn't a model release — it's Google paying SpaceX $920M/month for xAI compute, which redraws the map of who controls AI's physical substrate. On the tooling side, the Python JIT pause signals that CPython's optimization ambitions are hitting political and resource limits.

- [Google to pay SpaceX $920M a month for compute capacity at xAI data centers](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48417490) — 118pts — worth exploring: what does it mean for AI independence when Google depends on Musk-affiliated infrastructure?
- [Python JIT project was asked to pause development](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48425982) — 139pts — the inside story of CPython optimization politics has real implications for Python's competitiveness as AI's default language

**agent-systems**: The "agentic PC era" framing is getting stress-tested on two fronts: Nvidia's beastly CPU proposal for Windows and a wave of MCP/sub-agent tooling in Show HN. But the Meta Instagram hack — where attackers weaponized an AI chatbot to compromise thousands of accounts — is the cautionary data point that agentic systems with real permissions are a live attack surface.

- [Meta confirms 1000s of Instagram accounts were hacked by abusing its AI chatbot](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48427643) — 347pts — the first major documented case of an AI assistant being weaponized at scale for account takeover; read this before you ship any agent with account access
- [My Agent Skill for Test-Driven Development](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48398925) — 219pts — concrete workflow for agents writing tests first; one of the more grounded "agents in practice" pieces in a sea of hype

**Deep dives**:
- [How LLMs work](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48389360) — 836pts with 232 comments suggests it's unusually clear and usable as a teaching resource; worth bookmarking for the next time someone asks you to explain transformers
