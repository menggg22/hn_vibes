## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Compression and efficiency are the dominant research directions — Gemma 4 QAT and the transformer architecture study both ask "do we need all of this?" rather than "how do we scale up?" On-device viability is becoming a real engineering question, not a research fantasy.
- [Gemma 4 QAT models: Optimizing compression for mobile and laptop efficiency](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48414653) — 235pts — QAT (Quantization-Aware Training) rather than post-hoc quantization is the meaningful distinction here; worth reading for what Google learned about quality-at-compression tradeoffs
- [Do transformers need three projections? Systematic study of QKV variants](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48405931) — 207pts — Systematic ablation of attention mechanism fundamentals; the answer affects every inference budget calculation going forward

**agent-systems**: The mood is corrective rather than expansive — builders are asking how to make agents less wrong, not more capable. The Claude/rsync story and the "I nerfed our agents" Show HN both point toward reliability engineering displacing capability chasing as the frontier concern.
- [Did Claude increase bugs in rsync?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48411635) — 269pts — The forensic framing (did it? how do we know?) is more important than the verdict; establishes a methodology for holding AI coding tools accountable
- [My Agent Skill for Test-Driven Development](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48398925) — 113pts — TDD as a constraint on agents rather than a post-hoc check; structurally interesting approach to the reliability problem

**Deep dives**:
- [Transformers Are Inherently Succinct](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48416635) — 75pts but 0 comments suggests a dense paper drop; worth reading before the discussion catches up
- [Entanglement Builds Space-Time. Now "Magic" Gives It Gravity](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48409675) — quantum gravity via "magic" (non-stabilizerness) is a genuine frontier result that rarely surfaces on HN with this much engagement
- [pg_durable: Microsoft open sources in-database durable execution](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48414367) — 281pts — Durable execution (à la Temporal) moving into the database layer has significant architectural implications; the Microsoft open-source angle adds trust questions worth reading the comments for
