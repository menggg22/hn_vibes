## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The infra conversation today is less about raw training and more about the messy middleware layer — workflow durability, context management, and the cost of unreliable agent execution. Postgres keeps appearing as the boring-but-correct substrate for things that used to require exotic infrastructure.
- [Building durable workflows on Postgres](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48313530) — 244pts — worth reading as a counter-narrative to "use a dedicated workflow engine"; the argument that Postgres is good enough for durable execution has real production teeth

**agent-systems**: Agent tooling is fracturing into micro-niches: rollback, context injection, permission UI, multi-agent debugging — each a tiny startup surface. The satirical game about permission fatigue (#2 Show HN) is doing more to crystallize the UX problem than most serious papers.
- [Dynamic Workflows in Claude Code](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48311705) — 134pts — 112 comments relative to 134 points means practitioners are actively debugging the pattern; read for the war stories in the thread

**Deep dives**:
- [Disagreement among frontier LLMs on real-world fact-checks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48307887) — 483pts 334c — if models disagree on facts, the whole "use LLMs for research" stack has a foundation problem; the comment thread is unusually empirical
- [Various LLM Smells](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48313810) — 173pts 131c — code-smell framing applied to LLM behavior is a useful mental model; read for the taxonomy which is starting to crystallize into shared vocabulary
- [Sam Altman and Dario Amodei are both walking back AI jobs apocalypse predictions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48314363) — 164pts 132c — the narrative whiplash from two of the most prominent AI voices is worth tracking; HN is parsing whether this is intellectual honesty or strategic repositioning ahead of regulatory pressure
