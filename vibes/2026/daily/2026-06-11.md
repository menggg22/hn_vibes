## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: On-device and efficiency-first ML is quietly maturing — DiffusionGemma's 4x text generation speedup and Magenta running without GPU on iPhone both point toward inference optimization becoming the next hardware battleground rather than raw model scale.
- [DiffusionGemma: 4x Faster Text Generation](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48478471) — 272pts — diffusion-based text generation as an alternative decoding paradigm is worth tracking; if the quality holds, this is an architectural bet against autoregressive dominance

**agent-systems**: Agent infrastructure is showing its rough edges publicly today — the Hyper-V VM footprint story and the €0.01 bank transfer exploit both expose that agent systems are being deployed at a layer of trust they haven't earned yet, and the attack surface is larger than anyone admitted.
- [A €0.01 bank transfer could compromise a banking AI agent](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48476136) — 157pts — prompt injection via transaction metadata is exactly the class of attack that's obvious in hindsight; the real story is that financial agents are already in production

**Deep dives**:
- [Apache Burr: Build reliable AI agents and applications](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48477400) — Apache foundation backing for an agent framework signals the space is mature enough for governance overhead; worth reading to understand what "reliable" means in practice here
- [Rich Sutton on AI creativity and discovery](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48470581) — Sutton's framing of creativity as a systems property rather than an emergent mystery is worth engaging with seriously on a day when everyone is arguing about what Fable can and can't do
- [Policy on the AI Exponential](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48480719) — 178 comments on an AI policy piece with only 120 points means the arguments inside are genuinely contested; high engagement-to-endorsement ratio suggests discomfort rather than agreement
