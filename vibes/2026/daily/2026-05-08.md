## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: On-device inference is quietly maturing — DeepSeek 4 Flash getting a Metal-optimized local engine and ZAYA1-8B matching R1-class math with sub-1B active parameters both point toward "capable-enough, runs-on-your-hardware" becoming a real category, not just a benchmark claim.
- [DeepSeek 4 Flash local inference engine for Metal](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48050751) — 259pts — Metal optimization for Apple silicon suggests the on-device inference war is now being fought at the GPU-backend layer, not just the model layer

**agent-systems**: The builder community is converging on the insight that prompts don't scale — control flow, evals, and sandboxed execution are the actual problems, and three separate stories today (agents need control flow, agent-skills-eval, Tilde.run) all attack this from different angles.
- [Agents need control flow, not more prompts](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48051562) — 289pts — the thesis that prompt-engineering is hitting a ceiling is gaining mainstream HN traction; worth reading as a position statement for where agent tooling goes next

**Deep dives**:
- [Natural Language Autoencoders: Turning Claude's Thoughts into Text](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48052537) — 168pts — mechanistic interpretability meeting practical compression; read this to understand what "thoughts" in an LLM actually compress to
