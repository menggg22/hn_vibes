## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Local inference is no longer a hobbyist curiosity — it's framed today as a security and sovereignty posture. The M4/24GB post and the local-AI manifesto together suggest the community is building a coherent argument, not just running benchmarks.
- [Running local models on an M4 with 24GB memory](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48089091) — 534pts — concrete memory/throughput numbers on consumer hardware; the interesting question is where the cliff is between "good enough" and "needs cloud"
- [Training an LLM in Swift, Part 1: Taking matrix mult from Gflop/s to Tflop/s](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48085685) — 208pts — Swift as an ML training substrate is genuinely underexplored; the Apple-silicon alignment makes this more than a curiosity
- [CUDA-oxide: Nvidia's official Rust to CUDA compiler](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48096692) — 347pts — Nvidia blessing Rust officially changes the calculus for anyone writing GPU kernels who's been waiting for permission

**agent-systems**: The tone has shifted from "can agents do X" to "what does production agent infrastructure actually cost and who owns it." Maintenance, billing, and trust are the new questions.
- [An AI coding agent, used to write code, needs to reduce your maintenance costs](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48089289) — 347pts — frames agent value not as feature output but as maintenance-cost reduction; a maturation of the conversation
- [Natural-language messages between LLM agents are an architectural anti-pattern](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48099171) — 14pts — low score but the thesis is sharp: NL inter-agent messages are ambiguous, unversioned, and untestable — worth reading before building

**Deep dives**:
- [Incident Report: CVE-2024-YIKES](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48086082) — 678pts 167c — the name alone signals self-awareness; worth reading for how the vulnerability was disclosed and what "responsible disclosure in 2026" actually looks like
- [Mythos Finds a Curl Vulnerability](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48091737) — 607pts 251c — AI-assisted vuln discovery in one of the most-audited C codebases alive; the methodological story is more important than the CVE itself
