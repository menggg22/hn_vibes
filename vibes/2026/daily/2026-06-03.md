## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Microsoft is quietly building a model family (MAI-Code-1-Flash, MAI-Thinking-1) that's landing on the front page with real scores, suggesting the Azure/MAI stack is gaining credibility as an alternative to OpenAI. Meanwhile, AMD MI300X deployments of DeepSeek signal that the non-NVIDIA inference stack is becoming production-viable.
- [Bringing Up DeepSeek-V4-Flash on AMD MI300X](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48373675) — 72pts — worth reading for the specific engineering pain points of non-CUDA deployment; the gap between "works" and "works well" on AMD is the real story

**agent-systems**: The agent tooling layer is fragmenting into micro-libraries ("give your agent claws," "150-line CLI," MCP registries) rather than consolidating — builders are rejecting heavy frameworks and reassembling primitives. The RSS-for-agents angle is emerging as a recurring framing.
- [Now AI agents need what RSS does](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48375673) — 44pts — provocative analogy that frames the agent discovery/subscription problem as a solved-but-forgotten protocol problem; could spark a retro-standards revival

**Deep dives**:
- [A walking tour of surveillance infrastructure in Seattle (2020)](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48369980) — resurfaces today because it pairs perfectly with the age-verification debate; physical surveillance infrastructure and digital identity infrastructure are converging and this is a grounding document
