## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The cost-efficiency angle is dominating over raw capability — Norway's 2PB Huawei flash storage for LLM training and DeepSeek's high-caching low-cost agent both signal that the infrastructure conversation has shifted from "what can it do" to "what does it cost to run at scale." The Forge guardrails result (8B model hitting 99% with constraints) reinforces that smaller, cheaper models with smart scaffolding are beating frontier brute force.
- [Norway's 2 petabytes of Huawei flash storage and LLM training](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48270770) — 126pts — flash storage at petabyte scale for training is a real infrastructure bet worth understanding; what's the latency/cost tradeoff vs. spinning disk or HBM?

**agent-systems**: Reliability and cost are the twin obsessions — not new capabilities. DeepSeek's coding agent is exciting for its caching architecture, while Forge's guardrails story proves the community believes constrained smaller models beat unconstrained large ones for production agentic work. The Chert iMessage API play also signals that agents need reliable messaging infrastructure, not just model APIs.
- [DeepSeek reasonix, DeepSeek native coding agent with high caching and low cost](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48256953) — 706pts — caching architecture in reasoning agents is underexplored; if prompt caching cuts costs dramatically, what does that do to the economics of long-horizon coding tasks?

**Deep dives**:
- [Bytecode VMs in surprising places (2024)](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48236122) — 137pts — relevant today because agent runtimes and tool-use sandboxes are quietly reinventing bytecode VM patterns; worth reading as infrastructure substrate for agents
- [White Rabbit – sub-nanosecond synchronization for large distributed systems](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48245916) — 187pts — as AI inference clusters grow to thousands of nodes, sub-nanosecond distributed sync becomes load-bearing; this is the unsexy infrastructure that makes scale possible
- [A fundamental principle of aeronautical engineering has been overturned](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48260117) — 227pts — worth reading as a reminder that "settled" engineering assumptions collapse when measurement fidelity improves; directly applicable mindset to ML benchmarking orthodoxy
