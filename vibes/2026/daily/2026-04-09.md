# HN Vibes — 2026-04-09

**Top themes**: AI trust & control, security/privacy erosion, geopolitics-meets-crypto, retro hacking culture, right-to-repair

**Vibe**: HN is in a deeply anxious mood about who controls critical infrastructure — from Sam Altman's unchecked power to Microsoft killing VeraCrypt updates to Iran demanding Bitcoin tolls at Hormuz; the old hacker ethos of autonomy and trust is visibly fraying, and people are angry about it.

**Standout**: [Sam Altman may control our future – can he be trusted?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47659135) — the single highest-energy post of the day at 2081pts and 881 comments, a rare combination of score and depth indicating genuine collective anxiety about AI power concentration, not just curiosity.

**Show HN lesson**: [Is Hormuz open yet?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47696562) nailed it by anchoring a technical project directly to a live breaking news situation — real-world urgency converts browsers into voters faster than any feature list.

## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The headline claim today is MegaTrain's assertion that 100B+ parameter full-precision training is now feasible on a single GPU — if true (and HN will want receipts), it significantly changes the cost calculus for researchers without cluster access. Apple Silicon fine-tuning tooling (Gemma 4) continues maturing quietly on the Show HN side, suggesting on-device ML is graduating from demo to workflow.
- [MegaTrain: Full Precision Training of 100B+ Parameter LLMs on a Single GPU](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47689174) — 264pts — the specific claim of "full precision" (not quantized) at this scale is what makes this worth scrutinizing; most single-GPU training tricks involve precision tradeoffs

**agent-systems**: Two distinct threads today — enterprise-facing (Project Glasswing securing AI-era software supply chains, Muse Spark's "personal superintelligence" pitch) and builder-facing (TUI-use, Skrun, the Claude Code parallel server Show HN). The enterprise thread is generating more controversy because it's making larger promises; the builder thread is generating more genuine excitement because it's shipping.
- [Muse Spark: Scaling towards personal superintelligence](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47692043) — 286pts — 299 comments on 286 points is near 1:1, meaning this is generating as much skepticism as enthusiasm; worth reading the thread for the clearest current articulation of what "personal superintelligence" critics actually object to
