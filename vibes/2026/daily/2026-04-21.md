## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The inference performance race is intensifying at the hobbyist layer — 207 tok/s on a 3090 for a 27B model is a benchmark people can actually reproduce at home, and Apple Silicon is being treated as a serious inference platform not just a curiosity. Vendor verification (Kimi's verifier) is emerging as a new category as model providers proliferate and correctness diverges.

- [We got 207 tok/s with Qwen3.5-27B on an RTX 3090](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47838788) — 150pts — sparks curiosity: what's the ceiling for consumer-GPU inference, and how close are we to real-time 70B?
- [Kimi vendor verifier – verify accuracy of inference providers](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47838703) — 142pts — as inference gets commoditized, correctness auditing becomes the differentiator; this is early infrastructure for a coming trust layer

**agent-systems**: Agentic architectures are hitting a cost wall that's driving builders toward local message-passing schemes and runtime guardrails rather than pure cloud API chains. The "Claude co-authored-by" Tell HN signals that agent output is leaking into professional artifacts in ways teams haven't governed yet.

- [A lightweight way to make agents talk without paying for API usage](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47828968) — 50pts — sparks curiosity: if inter-agent communication can be decoupled from LLM calls, what does a fully local multi-agent topology look like?
- [Show HN: AI Coding Agent Guardrails enforced at runtime](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47837630) — 4pts — early but points at a real gap: runtime policy enforcement for agents, not just prompt-level instructions

**Deep dives**:
- [Quantum Computers Are Not a Threat to 128-Bit Symmetric Keys](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:47836784) — 100pts — read today to calibrate your actual post-quantum threat model vs. the vendor FUD cycle
