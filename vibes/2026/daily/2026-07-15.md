## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: On-device inference at the 27B scale is the headline, but the quieter story is the CUDA portability piece — running GPU workloads on non-Nvidia hardware is gaining real traction as supply constraints and licensing costs bite. The two stories together suggest the inference stack is rapidly decentralizing both upward (phones) and sideways (non-Nvidia silicon).
- [Bonsai 27B: A 27B-Class model that runs on a phone](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48910545) — 370pts — what quantization technique and context window tradeoff made phone deployment viable at this scale is the question to chase in the comments
- [Alternative(s) to run CUDA on non-Nvidia hardware](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48903715) — 130pts — which alternatives have reached production-viable maturity vs. which are still science projects is exactly what this thread is sorting out

**agent-systems**: The meta-conversation about agents has split into two distinct camps today — the architectural ("coding agents think ahead of time") and the economic ("zero-cost fallacy for open-source in the agentic era"). Builders are past "will agents work" and into "what does running agents at scale actually cost and who bears it."
- [Coding agents think ahead of time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48905764) — 88pts — whether "thinking ahead" is a genuine architectural shift or just longer context windows rebranded is the productive argument here
- [The zero-cost fallacy: open-source software in the agentic era](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48865001) — 95pts — agents making millions of automated dependency calls break the assumptions that made open-source economics work; worth reading before building any agent-heavy stack

**Deep dives**:
- [Cursor 0day: When Full Disclosure Becomes the Only Protection Left](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48910676) — 215pts 90c; a security disclosure about a tool that millions of developers now run with broad filesystem access is directly relevant to anyone using AI coding assistants today
- [Measuring Input Latency on Linux: X11 vs. Wayland, VRR, and DXVK](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48909424) — 337pts 216c; rigorous hardware measurement methodology on a contested topic — the methodology section alone is worth reading for anyone doing systems benchmarking
- [Financing the AI boom: from cash flows to debt](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48913443) — 54pts; the structural question of how GPU capex gets financed and what happens when the debt comes due is underread relative to its importance — low comment count means the thread hasn't been contaminated yet
