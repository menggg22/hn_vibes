*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: On-device inference is quietly maturing past the "proof of concept" phase — Nativ shipping frontier open model support for Mac and the AMD Zen 3 FP32 optimization post together suggest the frontier is moving to consumer hardware faster than the cloud-first narrative acknowledges.
- [Nativ: Run frontier open models locally on your Mac](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48982681) — 154pts — worth watching which models it supports and whether it's hitting the quality bar that killed earlier local-first apps
- [85.3 GFlops: Optimizing FP32 Matrix Multiplication on a Single AMD Zen 3 Core](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48947739) — 39pts — the low-level micro-optimization work that makes on-device inference actually viable; underrated post given how directly it feeds the local ML trend

**agent-systems**: Agent economics are becoming a real design constraint — "agent swarms and the new model economics" and the exploit-finding GPT5.6 story both show that the cost structure of running many agents changes what's worth building, with security being the clearest early stress test.
- [Agent swarms and the new model economics](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48982535) — 94pts — the framing of "swarms" shifting cost curves is the thing to stress-test; how does pricing change when you're running hundreds of cheap calls vs. one expensive one
- [Exploit brokers pay $500k for WordPress RCEs. I found one with GPT5.6 and $25](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48975665) — 379pts — the $25 input cost vs. $500k output value is the starkest agent-economics data point on the board today

**Deep dives**:
- [How we measured AI writing across arXiv, and where the measurement breaks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48981206) — read this alongside the China open-weights story; if measurement of AI writing is already breaking, the epistemic tools for tracking the open-weights race are also unreliable
