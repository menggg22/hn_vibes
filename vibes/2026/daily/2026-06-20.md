## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The dominant signal is a quiet shift toward cost accountability — "companies rein in AI usage" and the massive local-model Ask HN thread together suggest that on-device and self-hosted ML is moving from hobbyist curiosity to budget-driven necessity. The community is stress-testing whether local models have crossed the "good enough for daily coding" threshold.
- [Ask HN: Has anyone replaced Claude/GPT with a local model for daily coding?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48542100) — 1304pts — the 558-comment depth means real benchmark comparisons and workflow configs are buried in there; worth mining for ground truth on current local-model capability

**agent-systems**: MCP (Model Context Protocol) is quietly becoming the connective tissue of builder-land — two independent Show HNs ship MCP-adjacent tooling on the same day, and Zero-Touch OAuth for MCP is sitting at 263pts, suggesting the protocol is past "interesting idea" and into "people are building production systems on it."
- [Zero-Touch OAuth for MCP](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48592163) — 263pts — credential management for MCP agents is a genuine unsolved UX problem; this framing ("zero-touch") suggests the auth friction is the current bottleneck for agent adoption

**Deep dives**:
- [Project Valhalla, Explained: How a Decade of Work Arrives in JDK 28](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48595511) — value types and primitive classes finally in the JVM; read this today if you want to understand what the Java ecosystem will look like for the next decade
