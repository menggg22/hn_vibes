## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The Flash-tier model race (Gemini 3.5 Flash, Gemini Omni) is compressing the cost curve fast enough that on-device and constrained-inference use cases are becoming newly viable; meanwhile guardrail tooling is emerging as its own infrastructure layer sitting between raw models and production agents.
- [Show HN: Forge – Guardrails take an 8B model from 53% to 99% on agentic tasks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48192383) — 204pts — raises the question of whether guardrails are a product category or will be absorbed into inference runtimes

**agent-systems**: Builders are converging on the token-efficiency and observability problems rather than the model itself — the assumption that agents will run continuously at scale is baking in cost pressure from day one.
- [Show HN: Semble – Code search for agents that uses 98% fewer tokens than grep](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48169874) — 439pts — if 98% token reduction on a single tool call is real, it reframes what the agent compute budget actually pays for
