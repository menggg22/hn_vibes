## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: On-device and edge inference keeps quietly advancing — today's signal is in the Show HN layer (EdgeRunner for Swift/Metal, the REM sleep camera) rather than any big announcement, suggesting the frontier has moved to application builders rather than infrastructure writers.
- [Show HN: EdgeRunner – run GGUF models with Swift and Metal](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48795979) — 2pts — worth watching: Metal-native GGUF inference could be the cleanest path to truly offline iOS ML apps without Python bridges

**agent-systems**: The agent space today is fragmented and anxious — multiple builders shipping context-bridging, uncertainty-checking, and proof-requiring tools suggest that raw agent capability is not the bottleneck; reliability and trust are.
- [Show HN: Make No Mistakes – AI coding agents must prove their work](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48797101) — 4pts — the framing "agents must prove their work" is the right direction; formal verification meets LLM output, worth watching for whether it gets real traction

**Deep dives**:
- [Introduction to Compilers and Language Design (2021)](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48793454) — 264pts, resurfaced with strong score; if you've been putting off understanding how languages actually work, this is the moment the community is reading it
