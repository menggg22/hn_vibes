**Top themes**: frugal infrastructure, AI disillusionment, platform control, creative hacking, geopolitics-meets-tech

**Vibe**: HN is in a reflective, slightly defiant mood — celebrating builders who do more with less (the $20/month stack, EU-only SaaS, small models beating big ones) while quietly losing faith in platform giants (OpenAI removing features, Anthropic slashing cache TTLs, Google censoring games). ## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The story today isn't new model releases — it's the infrastructure layer cracking. Anthropic's silent cache TTL cut (461pts, 355c) and Claude Opus 4.6's hallucination regression (30pts) suggest the cost/quality tradeoff is getting harder to hide. Meanwhile, small models matching big-model security research is the most technically interesting counter-narrative.

- [Small models also found the vulnerabilities that Mythos found](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47732020) — 1228pts — if small models can do serious security research, the ROI calculus on frontier model spend is genuinely in question

**agent-systems**: Benchmark gaming is the dominant story — HN is waking up to the possibility that the leaderboards measuring agent capability are themselves compromised. Builders are still shipping agent wrappers (Claudraband, Chunk) but the epistemic foundation is shakier.

- [Exploiting the most prominent AI agent benchmarks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47733217) — 488pts — concrete exploitation techniques against the metrics everyone uses to justify agent investment; required reading before trusting any benchmark claim

**Deep dives**:
- [Anthropic downgraded cache TTL on March 6th](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47736476) — silent infra changes by AI providers are a new category of risk for builders; this thread is a masterclass in how to audit your API costs
- [The Closing of the Frontier](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47742790) — with tech valuations back to pre-AI levels (47745120), this piece on frontier-closing is the philosophical frame the data is pointing toward
- [Toffoli gates are all you need](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47672718) — reversible computing meets the universality debate; worth reading alongside the "small models are enough" thread for a unified theory of computational minimalism
