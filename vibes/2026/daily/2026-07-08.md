## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Local inference is graduating from "interesting demo" to "default preference" — Kokoro TTS at 245pts, a 1-bit WebGPU LLM, and Rowboat as a local Claude Desktop alternative all landed the same day, reflecting a coherent shift away from API dependency. The driver isn't just privacy; it's cost and latency at the edge.
- [Local, CPU-Friendly, High-Quality TTS with Kokoro](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48821576) — 245pts — worth benchmarking against cloud TTS on your own hardware today; the quality gap may have closed

**agent-systems**: The builder conversation has moved from "can agents do X" to "how do we know what the agent did and how much did it cost" — Halo (tamper-evident evidence), Context Warp Drive (deterministic folding), and Docx-CLI (token efficiency) all address production reliability, not capability. Auditability is the new feature.
- [Show HN: Halo – open-source, tamper-evident runtime evidence for AI agents](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48818098) — 25pts — the first credible open-source attempt at cryptographic agent audit trails; worth reading the architecture before this pattern gets standardized

**Deep dives**:
