## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Open-weight model quality is visibly compressing the gap with frontier closed models — Echo's Fable-parity claim and the Cactus calibration work both suggest the serious infra conversation is shifting from "which API" to "which weights and what runtime." The Oracle layoff story adds a cautionary data point about what happens when infra bets are made at scale before the economics are proven.
- [Echo – Fable-level results at 1/3 the cost using open-weight models](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49026810) — 185pts — worth pressure-testing the benchmark methodology; if replicable, changes the calculus for any team currently paying frontier API rates

**agent-systems**: The credential and secrets problem is becoming the dominant unsolved agent-infrastructure concern — two independent projects shipped today to address it, which is a reliable signal that the pain is real and no default solution exists yet. Meanwhile, Screenpipe's "record how you work → agents" pitch represents a more ambitious frame: agents bootstrapped from human behavioral replay.
- [Launch HN: Screenpipe (YC S26) – Record how you work and turn that into agents](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49024620) — 51pts — the behavioral cloning angle is conceptually ahead of most agent tooling; worth watching whether the privacy model is credible
