## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The on-device inference story is getting sharper — Bonsai hitting 442T/s on M4 Max with ternary weights suggests that model architecture choices (not just hardware) are becoming the lever. OpenAI's latency post on the front page is the enterprise counterpoint: hyperscaler infra is optimizing for voice latency at scale while indie builders optimize for cost-zero local deployment, and these are converging on different users.
- [How OpenAI delivers low-latency voice AI at scale](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48013919) — 218pts — the architectural choices for sub-300ms voice pipelines are directly applicable to anyone building real-time agent interfaces

**agent-systems**: The Ask HN about abandoning agentic loops for deterministic systems is the canary — practitioners are quietly retreating from full autonomy. The "Agent Skills" post (50pts, 9c — suspiciously few comments for the topic) suggests the community has heard the pitch and wants receipts, not frameworks.
- [Agent Skills](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48015397) — 50pts — low comment engagement on an agent post is itself signal; the burden of proof for new agent abstractions just went up

**Deep dives**:
- [Microsoft Edge stores all passwords in memory in clear text, even when unused](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id:48012735) — 362pts — a 2026 browser doing this is either a deliberate architectural decision or a staggering oversight; the technical post-mortem thread will be illuminating
