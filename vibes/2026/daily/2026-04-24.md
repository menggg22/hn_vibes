## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The Qwen3.6-27B result is the most technically interesting data point today — flagship-level coding in a dense 27B model suggests the efficiency frontier is moving fast enough that "you need a 70B+" is becoming obsolete; meanwhile TorchTPU signals Google is serious about making TPUs a first-class PyTorch target rather than a JAX-only domain.
- [TorchTPU: Running PyTorch Natively on TPUs at Google Scale](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47881786) — 21pts — worth reading for what it reveals about Google's strategy to make TPUs competitive for the PyTorch ecosystem rather than ceding it entirely to CUDA

**agent-systems**: The agent credential and sandboxing problem is crystallizing as a real engineering discipline — Agent Vault and Endo Familiar both shipped today, and Claude Code's quality regression post is implicitly a debate about how much autonomy agents should have in the first place.
- [An update on recent Claude Code quality reports](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47878905) — 524pts — Anthropic responding publicly to quality degradation reports is unusual and worth reading as a signal about how model companies will handle production regressions going forward

**Deep dives**:
- [We found a stable Firefox identifier linking all your private Tor identities](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47866697) — 894pts — a deanonymization technique affecting Tor Browser users is serious; the technical mechanism (stable identifier in Firefox) is specific enough to be actionable reading
