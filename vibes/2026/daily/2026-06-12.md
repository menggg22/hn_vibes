## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Open reproduction of frontier models keeps accelerating — DeepSeek-R1 now has an open reproduction on the front page — while MiMo Code going open-source signals that coding-specific model releases are becoming a commodity. The benchmark wars feel increasingly hollow given Claude Fable's mid-tier coding results landing the same day Anthropic is apologizing for hidden guardrails.
- [Open Reproduction of DeepSeek-R1](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48489917) — 185pts — worth watching how close the reproduced evals get and what training compute delta looks like vs. original

**agent-systems**: The agent security surface is getting serious attention from builders today — secrets, egress, and stability detection all shipped in the last 24h. Meanwhile FablePool is the first credible experiment in using collective human intent (pooled money) as a steering mechanism for what agents actually build.
- [Show HN: FablePool – pool money behind a prompt, and Fable builds it in public](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48496539) — 165pts — the incentive-alignment angle is underexplored: does financial skin-in-the-game change output quality or just monetize hype?

**Deep dives**:
- [Shall we play a game? My AI nuclear simulation](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48495575) — 174pts 161c — read alongside the autonomous drone story; both are about AI making consequential decisions in adversarial environments and neither has a clean answer
- [Software is made between commits](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48492533) — 186pts 127c — as AI generates more of the committed code, the tacit knowledge that lives between commits is being systematically destroyed; this piece will feel prescient in 18 months
