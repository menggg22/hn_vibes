## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Inference acceleration is the dominant engineering story right now — DSpark's speculative decoding paper is pulling serious comment volume, and smaller items like KV-psi (PSI-based cache eviction) and the llama.cpp TPS patch suggest practitioners are squeezing every cycle on commodity hardware. The frontier is shifting from "can we run it" to "can we run it cheap enough to matter."
- [DSpark: Speculative decoding accelerates LLM inference](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48696585) — 714pts — worth reading for the draft-model selection methodology, which is where most speculative decoding implementations quietly fail

**agent-systems**: The agent tooling layer is filling in fast but unevenly — Adrafinil solving "keep my laptop awake while Claude works" is a tell that real agentic workflows are running on consumer hardware right now, while the Mythos access restrictions story implies frontier-agent deployment is becoming a geopolitical chokepoint. The mundane and the geopolitical are converging.
- [Show HN: Adrafinil – keep a lid-closed Mac awake only while agents work](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48701512) — 71pts — the existence of this utility is more informative than its code; it means people are leaving agents running overnight on MacBooks

**Deep dives**:
