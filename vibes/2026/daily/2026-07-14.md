## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Apple's SpeechAnalyzer API landing with benchmark coverage signals the on-device inference story is maturing fast — platform-native APIs are starting to compete seriously with open-source pipelines. The "e-waste GPU" benchmarking story underneath it shows the community is simultaneously squeezing value out of old iron.
- [Apple's new SpeechAnalyzer API, benchmarked against Whisper and its predecessor](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48894752) — 412pts — worth reading for the latency and accuracy tables; if Apple's on-device ASR is within striking distance of Whisper, the self-hosting calculus shifts

**agent-systems**: Multiple converging signals today — Clawk for isolation, kassette for durability, Jacquard for human-in-the-loop review — suggest the community has moved past "can agents code?" to "how do we make agents safe and auditable?" Clawk's strong showing confirms sandbox-first architecture is the new consensus.
- [Clawk – Give coding agents a disposable Linux VM, not your laptop](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48892859) — 172pts — the architecture (disposable VM per session, not a container with your home dir mounted) is worth stealing even if you don't use the product

**Deep dives**:
- [GhostLock, a stack-UAF that has existed in all Linux distributions for 15 years](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48834309) — read this to understand how a locking primitive can have a 15-year-old use-after-free; the disclosure timeline and patch complexity are a masterclass in kernel security response
- [Building and shipping Mac and iOS apps without ever opening Xcode](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48896665) — 255pts 116c — the workflow details matter: if this is reproducible, it significantly lowers the barrier for non-Mac-native developers shipping Apple platform apps
