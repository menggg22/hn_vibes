## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The inference efficiency race is intensifying below the model layer — multiple projects today are about squeezing more tokens per second out of existing hardware rather than waiting for bigger GPUs. Liquid AI's MoE architecture trained on 38T tokens is the research side; Tiny-vLLM and the 3k tokens/s post are the production side.
- [Real-time LLM Inference on Standard GPUs: 3k tokens/s per request](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48321076) — 193pts — the claim of 3k t/s on *standard* GPUs challenges the assumption that serious inference requires H100s; worth verifying the methodology

**agent-systems**: MCP is apparently dead (or at least someone thinks so), and "Dynamic Workflows in Claude Code" is getting real engagement — the field is consolidating around fewer, more opinionated primitives after a year of protocol proliferation. SQLite as a workflow durability backend is the pragmatic counter-move to complex orchestration stacks.
- [SQLite is all you need for durable workflows](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48326802) — 308pts — provocative thesis that the simplest possible persistence layer is sufficient for most workflow durability needs; good foil to the Temporal/heavy-orchestration camp

**Deep dives**:
- [The mysterious Hy3 LLM is topping OpenRouter Model Rankings by a large margin](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48317294) — a model nobody can identify is beating known models on public rankings; the mystery itself is the story and worth following for what it reveals about benchmark gaming or genuinely novel architecture
- [Liquid AI reveals 8B-A1B MoE trained on 38T](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48325306) — 38T training tokens on a sparse MoE at 8B total parameters; the ratio of data to active parameters here is unusual and the architecture choices deserve a close read
- [We should be more tired than the model](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48322118) — 144pts 129c — high comment engagement for what sounds like a philosophical piece; the ratio suggests this is landing as something real about the human cost of working alongside always-available AI systems
