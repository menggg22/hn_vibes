*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: Inference cost is coming back into focus after a period of "just throw GPUs at it" thinking — napkin math and reproducible benchmarks are the day's aesthetic, reflecting growing pressure on margins. The hallucination regression story adds urgency: if bigger models are less reliable, the whole scale-up thesis needs re-examining.
- [Inference cost at scale with napkin math](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48560227) — 47pts — back-of-envelope reasoning is regaining respect as LLM bills arrive; worth reading for the mental models alone

**agent-systems**: Cloudflare offering ephemeral accounts for AI agents is a meaningful infrastructure signal — the disposable-identity primitive is quietly becoming foundational for autonomous agents operating on the web. Meanwhile builders are shipping agent-adjacent tooling (loop engineering, workflow codegen) but engagement remains modest, suggesting the space is still pre-product-market-fit.
- [Temporary Cloudflare accounts for AI agents](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48608394) — 154pts — disposable identity + network access for agents; this is the boring infrastructure that actually makes autonomous agents viable at scale
