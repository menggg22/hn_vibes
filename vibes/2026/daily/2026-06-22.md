## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The community is doing first-principles hardware math rather than trusting vendor benchmarks — occupancy calculations on AMD MI355X and dual-model residency on DGX Spark both reflect a maturing, skeptical audience that wants to verify claims from the silicon up.
- [Two Qwen3 models on one DGX Spark: the residency math](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48587865) — 84pts — the specific question of whether two models fit simultaneously on shared memory is exactly the kind of deployment detail that matters in production and almost never appears in marketing sheets
- [Occupancy Math on the AMD MI355X: A From-First-Principles Guide](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48571136) — 42pts — AMD's ROCm ecosystem is maturing enough that deep occupancy analysis is now worth writing; signals real workloads moving off CUDA

**agent-systems**: Builders are converging on reliability and context management as the real unsolved problems — not capability, but predictability. Multiple posts today address the "explain yourself from scratch every session" tax and the question of whether agents can be trusted with real systems.
- [Building reliable agentic AI systems](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48615680) — 177pts — reliability framing over capability framing is a meaningful shift; the interesting thread is what failure modes people are actually hitting
- [Stop wasting tokens and re-explaining your project between sessions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48622590) — 54pts — the session-context problem is generating both editorial posts and Show HNs simultaneously, a strong signal it's acute

**Deep dives**:
- [Apertus – Open Foundation Model for Sovereign AI](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48622778) — worth reading today specifically against the Claude identity verification story; the sovereign AI framing is a direct response to perceived closed-system risk
