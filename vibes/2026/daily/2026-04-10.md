## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The conversation is shifting from "does it work" to "can we trust what it's doing with our data" — the Vercel/Claude prompt-reading incident and SynthID reverse-engineering both point to a demand for transparency at the infrastructure layer.
- [Reverse engineering Gemini's SynthID detection](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47709130) — 101pts — worth understanding how watermark detection actually works before the ecosystem standardizes on it

**agent-systems**: Agents are being scrutinized on two axes simultaneously — reliability (Claude mixing up speakers) and security (plugins reading prompts); the "read before you code" framing in Research-Driven Agents is a direct response to both failure modes.
- [Research-Driven Agents: When an agent reads before it codes](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47706141) — 123pts — the architectural pattern here (retrieval before generation) is worth stealing regardless of the hype framing
- [The Vercel plugin on Claude Code wants to read your prompts](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47704881) — 252pts — supply-chain trust problem in agent tooling; the plugin permission model has no established best practice yet

**Deep dives**:
- [How NASA Built Artemis II's Fault-Tolerant Computer](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47704804) — fault tolerance at this level is a rare concrete case study; useful contrast against how "reliable" consumer AI systems claim to be
- [How Pizza Tycoon simulated traffic on a 25 MHz CPU](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47703123) — constraint-driven simulation design; the techniques are genuinely applicable to modern resource-limited agent loops
- [BunnyCDN has been silently losing our production files for 15 months](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47710845) — 97pts — a slow-burn data loss incident with no alerting; important reading for anyone treating CDN as primary storage
