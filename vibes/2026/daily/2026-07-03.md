## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The benchmark arms race continues with CursorBench 3.1 and the new Senior SWE-Bench both dropping, while the "is one transformer layer enough?" paper suggests the field is productively questioning its own scaling assumptions. There's a quiet thread of minimalism running through today's ML stories.

- [Is One Layer Enough? A Single Transformer Layer Matches Full-Parameter RL Training](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48760201) — 136pts — if a single layer with RL matches full fine-tuning, the implications for on-device deployment and inference cost are significant; worth reading the methods closely

**agent-systems**: Benchmarks for agents-as-engineers are proliferating (CursorBench, Senior SWE-Bench) and the community is simultaneously building meta-tools to constrain, observe, and test agents — a sign the "raw agent" era is ending and the "production agent" era is beginning, complete with all the boring-but-necessary infrastructure that implies.

- [Senior SWE-Bench: open-source benchmark that assesses agents as senior engineers](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48755928) — 164pts — the framing shift from "can it write code?" to "can it reason like a senior engineer?" is the right next question; curious whether the benchmark actually captures judgment or just harder syntax tasks

**Deep dives**:
- [Since Linux 6.9, LUKS suspend stopped wiping disk-encryption keys from memory](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48763035) — a regression that silently broke a security guarantee for ~14 months; worth reading to understand how kernel subsystem interactions create invisible security surface
- [NSA tries to weaken mlkem standardisation?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48760490) — post-quantum crypto standardization under political pressure is the kind of slow-moving catastrophe that only HN reliably tracks; read before the thread ages out
- [The fall of the theorem economy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48758048) — 252pts, 108c — a cultural/intellectual piece about formal verification and proof culture; pairs interestingly with zkGolf and the JVM strict field initialization JEP also on the front page today
