## 🔭 Depth — ml-infra / on-device ML / agent-systems

*What's the trend and what sparks curiosity.*

**ml-infra & on-device ML**: The dominant thread today is running serious models on constrained or ancient hardware — a direct response to cloud inference costs and the proliferation of open weights. The community is increasingly treating "no GPU / old CPU" as a legitimate target environment, not a curiosity.
- [Running Gemma 4 26B at 5 tokens/sec on a 13-year-old Xeon with no GPU](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48922434) — 218pts — sparks the question: at what point does CPU inference become "good enough" to route away from cloud API spend entirely?

**agent-systems**: Two distinct sub-trends are converging — persistent memory for agents (what did the agent learn last session?) and compute/queue infrastructure for running agents at scale. Builders are past "hello world" agents and hitting real production pain.
- [Open-source memory for coding agents, synced over SSH](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48923111) — 103pts — SSH as the sync primitive is an interesting design choice; makes agent memory portable across machines without a cloud dependency
- [Towards a harness that can do anything](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48921077) — 163pts — the framing of "harness" rather than "framework" signals a shift toward thinking about agents as things that need containment and control, not just capability

**Deep dives**:
- [Mysteries of Telegram Data Centers (2022)](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48920475) — resurfacing alongside *Telegram Serverless* makes today a rare double-Telegram day; reading both back-to-back reveals the architectural gap between their public infra story and their actual serverless ambitions
- [Telegram Serverless](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48918534) — 174pts / 92c — Telegram building serverless primitives is under-covered; worth reading to understand whether they're becoming an application platform, not just a messenger
- [The Three-Second Theft: Why AI Voice Fraud Outruns Every Defence](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48920432) — 163pts / 211c — high comment ratio on a security piece means practitioners are disagreeing about what defenses actually work; the 3-second voice clone threshold is the specific number worth pressure-testing
